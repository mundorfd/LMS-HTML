Background

The year is 2014. Your client operates in the commercial compost industry. They are interested in global temperature change and how that may affect their business. They know that as an Industrial Engineer, you are adept at performing forecasting tasks. The client has provided you with a dataset of average global temperatures over a long period of time that they have compiled from various proprietary sources. The task at hand is to forecast how the year 2015 may unfold in terms of global temperatures.

Deliverables

1 Excel Workbook Requirements

The Excel workbook must contain a minimum of four worksheets:

  1. The "Read Me" sheet: Will have a description of each sheet and detailed instructions on how to use the model your team created
  2. Forecasting model: Show your forecasting model here. This could include instructions, an automated sheet for input, etc. (You may use more than one sheet; be sure to describe it clearly in the "Read Me" sheet)

2 Professional Memo

Format Requirements: Memo must be word-processed, 12-point fonts, 1-inch border all around, single spacing, and contain the following sections:

Required Sections:

  1. Executive summary
    Max. 1 page. Upon reading your Executive Summary, the reader should be able to understand your entire project without having to read through the entire memo
  2. Methodology (i.e. Method(s) selected with reasoning)
    Describe the methods used to analyze the problem, rationale in choosing the data to be used, forecasting method(s), decision criteria in deciding the best forecasting method(s) to use, and validating the forecasting model(s) created for this project
  3. Results
    Present your results in a way that allows readers to understand both the overall and individual results
  4. Recommendation
    What strategy do you recommend? What are the expected sales? What are the ranges? Recall the characteristics of a good forecast discussed in class and textbook, and make sure to incorporate those characteristics in your recommendations to your client

Memo Writing Tips

  • Use this template provided on Canvas: Project Portfolio Memo Template.docx
  • Use subsections. Ensure that your memo is well-organized and concise, getting to the point.
  • Use tables, bulleted lists, or figures whenever you can. However, be sure to provide a brief description to introduce the tables, lists, or figures before you display them.
  • Word economy is crucial; do not restate the problem, and do not state the obvious. Put yourself in your client’s shoes, and think about what your client would want to read in a memo.
  • If you want to include an appendix, think carefully about whether the appendix serves any purpose other than padding your memo. Do not include an appendix with only rows and rows of data. The Excel file of your work will provide this information.
  • Generally speaking, the weight of your memo and your project grade has an inverse relationship.

(Note: The client will not have a lot of time to read your memo.)
