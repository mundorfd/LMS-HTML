Getting Started with Research

Follow these essential steps to conduct effective academic research using library resources.

  1. 1 Define your research question

    Start by clearly articulating what you want to learn or prove. A well-defined research question will guide your entire research process and help you identify the most relevant sources.

  2. 2 Search library databases

    Use the library's online catalog and subject-specific databases to find scholarly articles, books, and other academic resources related to your topic.

  3. 3 Evaluate your sources

    Assess the credibility, relevance, and currency of each source. Look for peer-reviewed articles, reputable publishers, and current information that directly supports your research question.

  4. 4 Organize and cite your findings

    Create a systematic approach to organizing your sources and ensure proper citation format. Use citation management tools to keep track of your research materials.

Usage Guidelines

Best practices for implementing enhanced numbered lists effectively

When to Use Enhanced Numbered Lists:

  • Sequential Processes: Multi-step instructions that must be completed in order
  • Complex Tutorials: Detailed procedures with substantial explanations for each step
  • Assignment Instructions: Multi-part projects with distinct phases or requirements
  • Research Workflows: Academic or professional research methodologies
  • Course Navigation: Module or unit progression guides

Standard vs. Compact Guidelines:

  • Standard (40px badges): Use for primary instructional content and main procedures
  • Compact (30px badges): Use for quick references, checklists, and secondary content
  • Standard uses h3, compact uses h5 for proper hierarchy
  • Content Density: Standard for detailed explanations, compact for brief instructions

Accessibility Benefits:

  • Semantic Structure: Ordered list markup clearly indicates sequential content
  • Visual Hierarchy: Number badges and headings create clear content organization
  • Screen Reader Support: Proper heading structure aids navigation
  • High Contrast: White text on colored backgrounds meets accessibility standards

Maintenance Best Practices:

  • Consistent Numbering: Always start with 1 and increment sequentially
  • Color Coordination: Use consistent colors within a document or section
  • Copy Full Blocks: Include complete <li> structure when adding new steps
  • Test Responsiveness: Preview on mobile devices to ensure proper flow
  • Link Integration: Use consistent link colors that coordinate with badge colors
Usage Tip: Enhanced numbered lists work best for 3-10 steps. For longer processes, consider breaking into multiple sections or using your existing section dividers. Combine with other components like call-to-action boxes for related resources.
